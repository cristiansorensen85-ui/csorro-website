Why CSorro exists

Built from real operational chaos, not a template.

CSorro began because real teams do not work inside one neat app. Projects sit in Trello. Conversations sit in Discord or Slack. Files live in Drive. Reviews happen somewhere else. Invoices and reports are separate again. I wanted one clean operating system for the way modern teams actually work.

Production pressure

Managing recordings, assets, approvals, deadlines, communities and people taught me how quickly work gets messy when tools are disconnected.

Creator operations

Large creator projects need structure: briefs, teams, builders, editors, thumbnails, release plans, reviews and clear communication.

Business systems

The same problems exist in business: clients, projects, CRM, invoices, reports, files, meetings and team updates spread across too many places.
